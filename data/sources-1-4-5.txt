How Does A Meal Plan Work
Meal Plans are loaded onto your NYU student ID card, which works just like a debit card. Simply tap your card and the meal and/or Dining Dollars will automatically deduct from your account.
To sign up or check your meal plan balance, please visit this website.
USING YOUR MEAL PLAN IN TRADITIONAL DINING LOCATIONS: 
When you visit a traditional dining location, upon entry you will tap your card and one meal will be deducted. These locations are NYU Eats at Downstein, NYU Eats at Lipton, NYU Eats at Third North and Kosher Eatery.
USING YOUR MEAL PLAN IN RETAIL LOCATIONS:
All retail locations are à la carte. When visiting a retail location or pre-ordering with GrubHub, students have the option of using Dining Dollars or a Meal Exchange (one meal deduction/swipe). Meal Exchanges typically consist of an entrée, choice of fruit, side salad, bag of chips or side dish and a fountain drink. If buying items separately, Dining Dollars can be used instead of, or in addition to, a meal swipe. Meal Exchanges can be used at Marketplace at Kimmel, Upstein, Dunkin', Peet's Coffee, Jasper Kane Cafe and Palladium.  
HOW DINING DOLLARS WORK:
Dining Dollars can be used at all on-campus dining locations and have a dollar-for-dollar value. Students may use Dining Dollars for items like drinks and snacks.
Please note that unused Dining Dollars automatically rollover from the fall to spring semester only for active spring Meal Plan members. Dining Dollars do not rollover after the spring semester is complete. All campus dining locations accept dining dollars. You can also use Dining Dollars on the Grub Hub app.  
USING GRUBHUB ON CAMPUS:
Grubhub’s Campus Dining feature allows you to order ahead from some of the on-campus dining locations and pick up your food. You can use your meal plan and dining dollars on the Grub Hub app. To download follow the steps below.
1.         Download the Grubhub app & sign up
2.         Go to My Grubhub > Settings > Campus Dining
3.         Conﬁrm your campus & add your campus card via single sign-on
4.         Browse restaurants on campus and start ordering pickup.
Grubhub is accepted at the following locations for select options: Starbucks, Dunkin’, The Marketplace at Kimmel, Palladium, and Jasper Kane Café in Brooklyn and Upstein.

FAQ:
What is a Halal Diet?
In the most general sense, the word halal is used to describe those things that are religiously allowable for Muslims. A halal diet can be made up of cuisines of every kind so long as the ingredients utilized follow Islamic dietary guidelines.  The overall purpose is to provide a well-balanced, wholesome eating lifestyle that understands the relationship between spiritual growth and food consumption.  
Some of the main guidelines followed in a halal diet:
• no pork or pork by products.
• no blood, alcohol, or items cooked with alcohol.
• butchering of animals that includes hand-slaughtering
What is the Halal Certification Program?
Halal Food Standards Alliance of America (HFSAA) Halal Certification Program is a system to provide assurance that the food on your plate has been thoroughly vetted to meet a high standard of Halal which is inclusive of the personal Halal standards of the vast majority of people. HFSAA Standards require the following:
· The entire facility to be ritually cleansed and tested to confirm that there are no traces of porcine, alcohol, and other prohibited products remaining.
· Every product used to prepare meals to be thoroughly vetted by our team to be free of any prohibited ingredients.
· The HFSAA staff conducts regular audits to ensure that the facility is staying in adherence to HFSAA's standards, to train & educate employees, and to answer any questions.
Questions about the halal program? Please contact AskCampusServices@nyu.edu or 212-998-4900.

What is a Meal Plan?
A Meal Plan is a prepaid account students can use to buy meals on campus. Meal plans are loaded onto your NYU student ID card, which works just like a debit card at our dining locations.  These plans provide students with the convenience of not having to cook at home, buy groceries, or allot a certain amount of money for food each week.
Detailed information about NYU Meal Plans can be found on our website.

How does the meal plan work?
●      How does a Meal Plan work?
Once you have a Meal Plan, your student ID works like a debit card. Simply swipe/tap your card at the cash register and the meal (“swipe”) or dining dollars are deducted from your account automatically. There are three components to our Meal Plans: Meal Allowance, Meal Exchange, and Dining Dollars.
●      Meal Allowance (Meals or Swipes): This is the number of meals that can be used in traditional dining locations. The cashier will deduct one meal from your plan upon entering these locations. You can also take your Meal to Go at many locations, by simply informing the cashier upon entering. Some of our locations also allow pre-ordering through GrubHub,
●      Meal Exchange: This feature allows you to exchange one swipe for a combo meal at our retail locations: Marketplace at Kimmel, Upstein, Palladium, UHall, Dunkin’, Peet's Coffee and Tea, and Jasper Kane Cafe.
●      Dining Dollars: Dining Dollars are accepted at all dining locations. Dining Dollars work like a debit card; each time you make a purchase the total is subtracted from the balance in your account. Each Dining Dollars is equivalent to one U.S. Dollar. Dining Dollars are accepted at all NYU Dining locations.
 

Am I required to have a meal plan?
All residents, irrespective of academic status, assigned to traditional style residence halls and Othmer Hall in Brooklyn, as well as first-year, transfer and visiting students living in residence halls are required to participate in a meal plan.

How am I charged for my meal plan?
Students are charged for Meal Plans through their Bursar’s account. The charges are posted within 48 hours of the meal plan being assigned to your account. 

Are there exceptions to the mandatory meal plan policy?
 
There are no exceptions to the mandatory Meal Plan policy. However, if you believe you have a medical condition or disability that warrants an exception to the mandatory meal plan policy, you must submit a special accommodations form with supporting documentation to the Moses Center for Student Accessibility. For forms and additional information, visit the Moses Center for Accessibility and Inclusive Culture
 

Can I change or cancel my meal plan?
Allowable meal plan changes and cancellations may only be made through the meal plan portal at  mealplans.nyu.edu within the deadline for cancellations each semester. If you have questions, please contact us at AskCampusServices@nyu.edu.
If you sign up or change a meal plan membership after the beginning of a semester, the plan and charges are prorated for the remainder of that semester. Charges and refunds for all meal plans (meal swipes and Dining Dollars) are calculated on a daily basis. Charges begin on the activation date, not when the students begin using their plan.  
The same principle applies for changes and cancellations. Students pay for the number of days they are on their meal plan — whether used or not — and receive a credit or charge on their  Bursar’s account based on their change.
 

What happens if I do not use all of my meal swipes or Dining Dollars? Will they transfer (rollover) to the next term?
Any unused meals (swipes) expire at the end of each semester. For active spring meal plan members, unused fall Dining Dollars carry over from fall to spring semester but expire at the end of the spring semester. The meal plan participant must maintain an active meal plan in the spring semester in order to receive the fall Dining Dollars. The fall Dining Dollars are added after the Meal Plan posted spring change/cancel deadline.

How to Choose the Right Meal Plan*
Not sure which plan is right for you? Consider your schedule, eating habits, and how often you plan to dine on campus. Here’s a quick guide to help you decide:
8 or 10 Meal Plan: Great if you plan to eat on campus occasionally and may grab some meals off-campus. With an average of 1–2 meals per day, these plans work well if you prefer flexible dining.
12 Meal Plan: A balanced option with an average of 2 meals per day — perfect if you expect to eat most of your meals on campus while still having some flexibility for dining out.
17 or 21 Meal Plan: Ideal for those who want the convenience of having nearly all their meals covered on campus. With an average of 2–3 meals per day (17-meal plan) or 3 meals per day (21-meal plan), these options are best for a busy schedule and easy access to on-campus dining.
Every plan also includes $30 in Dining Dollars each week, giving you additional flexibility to grab snacks, coffee, or meals at select locations.

    Nutritional Support Initiatives As of Spring 2026
No Cost Meal Offerings
NYU Courtesy Meal Program: A short-term, emergency resource for students facing an unexpected financial disruption/situation and who cannot afford meals or groceries. Courtesy Meals:
grant a $75 dining dollars credit to a student's NYU ID
are offered free of charge
do not affect a student's financial aid
may be requested up to three times per academic year
are accepted only at NYU Dining locations in NYC.
Swipe it Forward: A collaboration between Student Government Assembly, Student Affairs, and NYU Dining that enables meal plan members to donate meal swipes to students experiencing food insecurity.
On Campus Food Pantries: NYU offers three on-campus food pantries to support students. 
For more information on all of these offerings, please visit this webpage. 
Value Programs
NYU Dining Deals: A range of affordable meal options available at Jasper Kane, Kimmel Marketplace, Upstein, Palladium, and the Market at Sidestein include:
$5 - Chips, Guacamole and bubbly
$5 - Fried Pickles with Bubbly
$7 - Mini Quesadilla served with Seasoned Rice and Bubbly
$7 - Biscuit Sandwich, Fries and Bubbly
For a full list of dining deals, please visit this webpage.
Ongoing Education and Outreach
Teaching Kitchens: Every month, NYU Dining conducts cooking classes that allow students to explore food, culinary, and nutrition literacy to positively impact their food choices. Simple recipes are used so students can prepare on their own.  
Free Nutritional Counseling Sessions: NYU Dining campus dietitian will offer sessions that counsel students on budgeting and grocery shopping, maximizing nutrient intake, special diets, disease management, etc. To learn more, please email: nyu.eats.nutrition@nyu.edu.
Farmer’s Market: A selection of fresh produce is delivered to NYU by eight local farm partners in the New York area. Students can use a meal plan swipe, dining dollars or credit to purchase a bag of produce.
Donations to the Local Community
City Harvest Food Donation Drive: Students are able to purchase a bag of non-perishables through NYU Dining (Sidestein Market and virtually through Grubhub) to be donated to City Harvest, towards the end of each semester.

Meal Plans
To get the most out of the first-year experience, NYU requires all first-year and transfer students, irrespective of academic status, living in residence halls to participate in an NYU Dining Meal Plan. If living on campus, you will automatically be assigned an NYU Meal Planopens in a new tab.
How does the meal plan work?
Eating on-campus is easy with an NYU Dining Meal Plan. We have multiple dining locationsopens in a new tab across campus that conveniently offer a variety of foods and delicious dining options. With a meal plan, you've got food covered, so it’s one less thing you have to worry about. Once you have a meal plan, your student ID works like a debit card. Simply swipe your card and the meal, meal exchange or dining dollars are deducted from your account automatically. There are three components to our meal plans: Meal Allowance, Meal Exchange, and Dining Dollars.
